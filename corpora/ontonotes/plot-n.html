plot-n; 4 Senses

Sense Number 1: a secret scheme to do something, especially something underhanded or illegal

Commentary: PLOT[+event]+[human][+thing_plotted]

Examples:
They concocted a plot to discredit the governor.
I saw through his plot from the start.
There's a plot to send Justin Bieber to North Korea!

Mappings:
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 1

Sense Number 2: a specific and measured piece or parcel of land, a small area of ground covered by specific vegetation, or a small piece or area of ground reserved for a specific purpose.

Commentary: PLOT[+event][+location][+specific_measurement][+specific_usage]

Examples:
At the Urban Gardens site, our vegetable plot has grown to over one acre.
Luxury burial plots in Jakarta have almost tripled to Rp 9.5 million in just under 3 years.
He built the house on a 78 acre plot of land.
Developers have opened bids on the plots, around the future site of a highrise and business park.

Mappings:
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 2

Sense Number 3: A storyline; the plan,scheme,or main story of a literary or dramatic work, true or fictional.

Commentary: PLOT[+storyline][+performance_or_written_account]

Examples:
The characters were well drawn but the plot was banal."
Little did he know that the plot of his very own life was to take a dramatic new twist.
The book's plot takes us alongside the river Nile, in the year 32 AD.

Mappings:
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 3

Sense Number 4: a chart or map showing the dimensions, movements or progress of something

Commentary: PLOT[+event][thing_plotted][+motion_or_dimension][+location]

Examples:
The police carefully triangulated his trajectory and issued an APB plot of his supposed whereabouts.
The plot of the new Spire Building includes a floor specially designated for department and convenience stores.
Attacks on targets were detected by communications intercept and accurately reported on a plot.

Mappings:
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 4
