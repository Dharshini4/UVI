bank-n; 11 Senses

Sense Number 1: a financial institution

Commentary: A money-holding institution.

Examples:
The bank holds the mortgage on my house.
I took my money out of the bank, and put it into stocks.
John switched from Chase Bank to PNC Bank.

Mappings:
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 1

Sense Number 2: the bank building

Commentary: Refers very narrowly to the building where banking takes place.

Examples:
The bank is on the corner or Nassau and Witherspoon.
It takes me ten minutes to walk to the bank.

Mappings:
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 1

Sense Number 3: sloping land

Commentary: Land that slopes downward, especially near a body of water or on a racetrack.

Examples:
I sat on the river bank and watched the currents.
We pulled the canoe up the bank.
There's a bank on this turn to reduce centrifugal force.

Mappings:
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 1

Sense Number 4: a supply of something

Commentary: A stock or reserve, usually for emergencies.

Examples:
The Red Cross created a blood bank for disaster relief.
They set up a food bank for the flood victims.

Mappings:
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 1

Sense Number 5: a container for holding money

Commentary: A kind of box, usually with a slot on the top.

Examples:
John smashed his piggy bank to buy comic books.
You have to put a quarter in the coin bank when you swear.

Mappings:
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 1

Sense Number 6: a row of objects

Commentary: An arrangement of similar things, usually in rows or tiers.

Examples:
He operated the bank of switches.
A bank of telephones is standing by.

Mappings:
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 1

Sense Number 7: gambling house funds

Commentary: The amount of money owned by a gambling house. Probably only the expression "break the bank" applies.

Examples:
He's hoping to break the bank at Monte Carlo.

Mappings:
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 1

Sense Number 8: a ridge or pile

Commentary: A land feature, a large amount of something on the ground.

Examples:
We climbed the sand bank with some difficulty.
A huge bank of earth lay between us.

Mappings:
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 1

Sense Number 9: a flight maneuver

Commentary: The act of a plane heading downward.

Examples:
The plane went into a steep bank.

Mappings:
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 1

Sense Number 10: ambiguous between institution and building

Commentary: Use this sense when you can't see a distinction between the first and second sense.

Examples:

I cashed my check at the bank.
