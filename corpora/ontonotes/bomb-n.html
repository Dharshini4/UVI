bomb-n; 4 Senses

Sense Number 1: An explosive devise.

Commentary: BOMB[+concrete]

Examples:
The Russians learned to make a bomb.
The bomb exploded outside a restaurant in Tel Aviv.

Mappings:
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 1

Sense Number 2: A strong vessel for measuring heat of combustion.

Commentary: BOMB[+concrete][+vessel]

Examples:
The lab has a bomb in the corner for experiments.

Mappings:
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 2

Sense Number 3: an event that fails badly.

Commentary: BOMB[+event][+negative]

Examples:
The first experiment was a real bomb.
The meeting was a bomb as far as new business was concerned.

Mappings:
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 3

Sense Number 4: something that is truly remarkable and perceived favorably.

Commentary: BOMB[+positive]
NOTE: must be "the bomb"

Examples:
She was the bomb last night.
Everyone thought the movie was the bomb.

Mappings:
