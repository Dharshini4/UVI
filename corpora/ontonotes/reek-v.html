reek-v; 3 Senses

Sense Number 1: Have an element suggest of something (but not related to smell).

Examples:
He reeks of corruption.
This passage reeks of plagiarism.
The campaign reeked of racism.

Mappings:

FrameNet: Appearance
PropBank: NM
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 1

Sense Number 2: Smell badly and offensively.

Examples:
The building reeks of smoke.
The countryside positively reeks.
She reeked of booze.

Mappings:
VerbNet: smell_emission-43.3
FrameNet: Chemical-sense_description
PropBank: reek.01
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 2

Sense Number 3: Emit a liquid, possibly as steam.

Examples:
The marshes reeked in the sun.
I reeked sweat from every pore.
