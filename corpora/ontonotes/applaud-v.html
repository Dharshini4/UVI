applaud-v; 2 Senses

Sense Number 1: clap hands together

Commentary: NP1[agent] APPLAUD (NP2[theme])
NOTE: compare to sense 2; this sense may be viewed as a subset, as clapping hands in one means of expressing approval; only literal, physical events of applause should be tagged with this sense

Examples:
Cheney was booed and Sheen was applauded. (passive)
Some of the crowd applauded before catcalls and boos could be heard.
She ended the day on a high note, as Simon and Paula both applauded her performance.
The candidate the audience was applauding enthusiastically went to the podium.
