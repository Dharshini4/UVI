gas-n; 5 Senses

Sense Number 1: flatulence

Commentary: GAS[+event] a state of excessive gas in the alimentary canal

Examples:
After eating the cheese, she had gas.
Don't eat beans before your interview, they'll give you gas.

Mappings:
WordNet 2.0 Sense Numbers: 4

Sense Number 2: accelerator

Commentary: accelerator pedal, gas pedal, throttle, gun - a pedal that controls the throttle valve

Examples:
He stepped on the gas and sped off down the road.
Her foot slipped off the gas and she caused an accident.
Is the gas on the left or the right?

Mappings:
WordNet 2.0 Sense Numbers: 5

Sense Number 3: gasoline

Commentary: gasolene, petrol - a volatile flammable mixture of hydrocarbons (hexane and heptane and octane etc.) derived from petroleum; used mainly as a fuel in internal-combustion engines

Examples:
Sport Utility Vehicles use a lot more gas than small cars.
We'll have to stop on the way because I'm low on gas.
The price of gas is always rising.

Mappings:
WordNet 2.0 Sense Numbers: 3

Sense Number 4: natural gas

Commentary: a fossil fuel in the gaseous state; used for cooking and heating homes

Examples:
In the winter they use a lot of gas ot heat their huge house.
My landlord pays for gas.

Mappings:
WordNet 2.0 Sense Numbers: 6

Sense Number 5: not liquid

Commentary: the state of matter distinguished form the solid and liquid states by: relatively low density and viscosity; relatively great expansion and contraction with changes in pressure and temperature; the ability to diffuse readily; and the spontaneous tendency to become distributed uniformly throughout any container

Examples:
At a certain temperature the substance turns in to gas.
Matter is something with mass and is a solid, liquid, plasma, or gas.
