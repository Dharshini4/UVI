peep-v; 3 Senses

Sense Number 1: Look furtively at something.

Examples:
He peeped at the woman through the window.
We peeped at the professor through her office window.
Stop peeping at me!

Sense Number 2: (Cause to) appear as if from hiding.

Examples:

The new moon peeped through the tree tops.
He peeped through the bushes.

Mappings:

Sense Number 3: Speak in a high-pitched tone of voice, as either an animal or human might.

Examples:

The teacher practically peeped at us from the blackboard.
He peeped throughout the meeting, lacking confidence.
