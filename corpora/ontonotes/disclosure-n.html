disclosure-n; 2 Senses

Sense Number 1: the act of revealing formerly secret information

Commentary: DISCLOSURE[+event][+communication][+speech_act][+revealing][+information][+secret]
NOTE: implies an agent in the act of communicating
NOTE: the DISCLOSURE of Y -> X DISCLOSES Y

Examples:
The judge ordered the disclosure of the government documents.
The disclosure of a terrorist plot by a man in custody has led to several more arrests.

Mappings:
PropBank: disclosure.01
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 1

Sense Number 2: information content that was secret, but now revealed

Commentary: DISCLOSURE[+entity][+symbolic][+information][+secret][+revealed]
NOTE: refers to the content of the information, not the act of communicating by an agent

Examples:
These shocking disclosures of infidelity in the papers are ruining the politician's career. Among the stolen documents may have been disclosures of new weapon technology.

PropBank: NM
WordNet 0.0 Sense Numbers: 2
