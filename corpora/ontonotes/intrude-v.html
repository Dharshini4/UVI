intrude-v; 2 Senses

Sense Number 1: enter uninvited or unlawfully

Commentary:
NOTE: This sense includes entering a physical place.

Examples:
They intruded on our dinner party.
She intruded into our sitting room.
I don't want criminals intruding on our land!

Mappings:
VerbNet: NM
FrameNet: NP
PropBank: NM

Sense Number 2: thrust oneself in as if by force

Examples:
My roommate intrudes on my privacy by reading my mail.
The colors don't intrude on the viewer.
