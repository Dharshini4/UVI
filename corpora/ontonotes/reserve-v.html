reserve-v; 2 Senses

Sense Number 1: withhold, keep

Commentary:
NOTE: "reserved" used as a modifier should be tagged as 'X', e.g. "He is kind but reserved" or "Get the sugar reserved as a topping"

Examples:
Please reserve your applause until the end of the performance.
I reserve the right to remain silent.
Drain the cherries, reserving the juice.
The USGS has reserved a licensing fee from the city of Wichita.
That sort of language should be reserved for the locker room.

Mappings:
VerbNet: NM
FrameNet: NP
PropBank: reserve.01
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 1, 2

Sense Number 2: book, register for

Examples:
He reserved a candlelit table for two.
You have to reserve rooms months in advance!
Please reserve me 3 tickets for the front row.
