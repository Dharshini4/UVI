reverberate-v; 4 Senses

Sense Number 1: ring or echo with sound

Commentary:
NOTE: Does not imply that sound is bouncing off of a particular surface.

Examples:
The singing reverberated through the chamber.
The hall reverberated with laughter.
I came back with a chop so loud that the noise reverberated throughout the building.

Mappings:
VerbNet: sound_existence-47.4
FrameNet: Sound_movement
PropBank: NM
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 1

Sense Number 2: Have a long or continuing effect.

Examples:
The case of a single 11-inch smallmouth bass has reverberated far
beyond a Maine lake and the fishing lodges of Maine's Grand Lake.
The discussions reverberated throughout his adult life.
While I had expected a certain level of cheesiness, there were also
some underlying themes that reverberated in my life.
Effects of the stock fall reverberated throughout the market.

Mappings:
VerbNet: NM
FrameNet: NM
PropBank: reverberate.01
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 2

Sense Number 3: Be reflected as heat, light, or shock waves;.

Commentary:
NOTE: Does not include sound as traditionally conceived, but includes sonar.

Examples:
Waves reverberated off the shore.
Sonar pulses reverberated along the hull.

Mappings:

FrameNet: Sound_movement

Sense Number 4: Process, or refine in a reverberating furnace

Examples:
The factory reverberates ore.
