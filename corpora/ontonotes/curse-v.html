curse-v; 2 Senses

Sense Number 1: Utter profanities (at).

Commentary:
NOTE: This sense MUST INCLUDE linguistic content. If there is nothing 'said,' use Sense 2. Includes: CURSE OUT

Examples:
The drunken men were cursing loudly in the street.
Mary cursed out her husband.
The taxi driver cursed at the passenger when he didn't pay.

Mappings:

FrameNet: NP
PropBank: curse.01
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 1, 2

Sense Number 2: Wish harm upon; invoke evil upon, possibly by excluding from a religious community.

Examples:
The bad witch cursed the child.
The church cursed the teens and sent them away.
Curse you!
Mary cursed her husband, wishing him to die.
