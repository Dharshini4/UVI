bundle-v; 4 Senses

Sense Number 1: Make into a bundle, wad, or cluster.

Commentary: INCLUDES: BUNDLE UP, BUNDLE INTO

Examples:
He bundled up his few possessions.
Please bundle the paper into the box.
She bundled her fingers into a fist.

Mappings:
VerbNet: shake-22.3-2-1
FrameNet: NP
PropBank: bundle.01
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 1, 2, 3
WordNet Verb Particle Constructions, Multiword Expressions:
bundle_up 1

Sense Number 2: Dress warmly.

Commentary: IS: BUNDLE UP

Examples:
Mother bundled up the children for the long way to school.
Grayhounds have to be bundled up in the winter time so they don't
catch cold.

Mappings:
VerbNet: NM
FrameNet: NP
PropBank: bundle.02,bundle.03
WordNet Verb Particle Constructions, Multiword Expressions:
bundle_up 2

Sense Number 3: Send off or to a destination unceremoniously; may be metaphorical.

Commentary: IS: BUNDLE OFF

Examples:
Charles Taylor gets bundled off home.
The students were bundled off to camp by grateful parents.
Cartloads of foreclosures are bundled off every day.

Mappings:
VerbNet: NM
FrameNet: NP
PropBank: NM
WordNet Verb Particle Constructions, Multiword Expressions:

Sense Number 4: Sleep fully clothed in the same bed with one's betrothed, possibly including the use of a bundling bag.

Examples:
Her parents allowed the couple to bundle for a night over the
vacation.
He accepted the invitation to stop by the house and be bundled for
the evening.
