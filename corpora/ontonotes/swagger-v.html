swagger-v; 2 Senses

Sense Number 1: Act in an arrogant manner, usually demonstrated by walking in a characteristic way.

Examples:
He swaggered up to the bar and ordered a drink.
He swaggers around like a rooster in a hen house.
She swaggers like her boyfriend, if not more.

Mappings:
VerbNet: run-51.3.2
FrameNet: Self_motion
PropBank: swagger.01
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 1, 3

Sense Number 2: Discourage or frighten with threats; intimidate.

Examples:
She swaggered him into giving up the money.
He well nigh swaggered her out of everything.
