rage-n; 3 Senses

Sense Number 1: A feeling or state of extreme anger.

Commentary: RAGE[+emotion]
NOTE: may be metaphorical (as of the elements or animals)

Examples:
His face turned red with rage.
The sea hurled itself in thundering rage.
My rage cannot be contained.

Mappings:
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 1, 2, 4

Sense Number 2: A feeling for something that is desired intensely.

Commentary: RAGE[+emotion][+directed]

Examples:
His rage for fame destroyed him.
Her rage for control led her to sorrow.

Mappings:
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 3

Sense Number 3: An interest followed with zeal, perhaps due to popularity.

Commentary: RAGE[+artifact]

Examples:
That skirt is all the rage this Spring.
His book was all the rage.
The rage that Fall was canvas shoes.

Mappings:
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 5
