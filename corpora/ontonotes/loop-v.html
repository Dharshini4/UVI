loop-v; 2 Senses

Sense Number 1: (Cause to) navigate in loops.

Commentary: Syntax Includes: NP1[agent] LOOP NP2[patient] NP1[theme] LOOP PP[directional]
NOTE: Does NOT include fastening as a result of the navigation.

Examples:
The bicycle looped around the tree.
The stunt pilot looped his plane.
He looped around the party a few times before asking her to dance.

Mappings:
VerbNet: other_cos-45.4
FrameNet: NP
PropBank: NM
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 1, 3

Sense Number 2: Wind around, fasten, or join with a loop.

Commentary:
NOTE: Must imply an object capable of fastening or wrapping by virtue of the looping action.

Examples:
He looped his watch through his belt.
Loop those ropes and set them on the deck.
She looped the ribbon through her daughter's hair.

Mappings:
VerbNet: coil-9.6,other_cos-45.4,tape-22.4
FrameNet: NP
PropBank: NM
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 2, 4, 5
