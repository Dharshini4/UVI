flower-n; 4 Senses

Sense Number 1: plant

Commentary: a plant cultivated for it's blooms or blossoms

Examples:
He grows flowers in the backyard.
Flowers always make a nice gift.
I took a class to learn to arrange flowers.

Mappings:
WordNet 2.0 Sense Numbers: 1

Sense Number 2: bloom

Commentary: blossom - the reproductive organ of angiosperm plants especially one having showy or colorful parts

Examples:
The flower of the plant is wilting.
Those who eat dandelions say that the flower is tastier than the stem.

Mappings:
WordNet 2.0 Sense Numbers: 2

Sense Number 3: prime

Commentary: FLOWER[+event] peak, heyday, bloom, blossom, efflorescence, flush - the period of greatest prosperity or productivity

Examples:
It was the flower or the golden age of cinema.

Mappings:
WordNet 2.0 Sense Numbers: 3

Sense Number 4: virginity

Commentary: FLOWER[+event] a euphemism for someone's virginity.

Examples:
You can't have my flower.
She was glad to have finally given him her flower.

Mappings:
WordNet 2.0 Sense Numbers: 3
