strike-n; 11 Senses

Sense Number 1: deliberate stoppage of an activity, especially work

Commentary: STRIKE[+action][+stoppage][+work][+deliberate] [+state][+inactivity][+work][+deliberate]

Examples:
The trains were on strike for six hours in Italy yesterday.
The prisoners organized a hunger strike.
The mechanics received strike pay from the union.
Can the tenants be evicted for going on a rent strike?

Mappings:
PropBank: strike.02
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 1

Sense Number 2: a sudden attack

Commentary: STRIKE[+action][+attack][+sudden]

Examples:
They ordered a pre-emptive strike on the terrorist stronghold.
A surgical strike was conducted against selected military targets, using smart bombs.
The government is trying to prevent another terrorist strike at home.

Mappings:
PropBank: strike.01
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 1

Sense Number 3: a valid baseball pitch that is missed

Commentary: STRIKE[+action][+pitch][+baseball][+valid][+missed] [+state][+disadvantage][+handicap]
NOTE: it is a handicap or black mark, because as the strikes accrue, the batter is in trouble.
NOTE: The above handicap situation is the source of the metaphoric extension of this sense.

Examples:
The count on the batter is now two balls and two strikes.
That fast ball went right over the inside corner of the strike zone.
California has a DWI policy of three strikes and you're out. (metaphoric extension)
These kids already have two strikes against them by the time they reach high school. (metaphoric extension, a disadvantage)
Bob's age was a strike against him when applying to medical school in his thirties. (metaphoric extension, a disadvantage)

Mappings:
PropBank: strike.03
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 5
