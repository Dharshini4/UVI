abide-v; 3 Senses

Sense Number 1: Dwell in some location, either physical or abstract.

Examples:
Abide with me.
He abided in her heart forever.

Sense Number 2: Put up with something or someone unpleasant; show deference towards someone or some authority.

Examples:
I cannot abide his constant criticism.
The new secretary had to abide a lot of unprofessional remarks.
She abided two years of a miserable marriage.
