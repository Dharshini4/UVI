domination-n; 2 Senses

Sense Number 1: the exercise of control over something

Commentary: DOMINATION[+activity][+exercise][+control][+decisive]

Examples:
This year's loss of the championship ended their team's six-year domination of the sport.
Thirty years of domination by a dictator had dismantled most of the nation's social institutions.
Wolf packs are hierarchical with the top wolf maintaining domination over all the other wolves.

Mappings:
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 1, 2

Sense Number 2: the fourth (of nine) ranks in the celestial hierarchy of angels

Commentary: DOMINATION[+relation][+rank][+fourth][+angelology][+Christian]
NOTE: always in the plural

Examples:
The dominations were the angels dwelling in the fourth region of the celestial hierarchy.

Mappings:
WordNet 0.0 Sense Numbers: 2
