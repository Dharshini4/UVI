lease-n; 3 Senses

Sense Number 1: Property that is leased or rented out or let.

Commentary: LEASE[+structure]

Examples:
The lease is available starting tomorrow.
This street is full of leases but very few are for sale.

Mappings:
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 1

Sense Number 2: A contract granting use or occupation of property under certain terms or conditions.

Commentary: LEASE[+contract]

Examples:
The lease will run for six months.
I'll draw up a lease this evening.
Sign the lease and return it to the address listed.

Mappings:
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 2

Sense Number 3: The period of time during which a leasing contract is in effect.

Commentary: LEASE[+time]

Examples:
The lease is up in a matter of days.
This lease will end on June 12.

Mappings:
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 3
