excitement-n; 5 Senses

Sense Number 1: a personal feeling of exhilaration

Commentary: EXCITEMENT[+state][+personal][+exhilaration]
NOTE: X[+recipient] HAS excitement

Examples:
The children's excitement grew noticeably as Christmas Eve approached.
We could barely conceal our excitement when they made the offer for our company.

Mappings:
PropBank: excitement.01
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 1

Sense Number 2: a personal state of agitation, upset

Commentary: EXCITEMENT[+state][+personal][+agitation]
NOTE: X[+recipient] HAS excitement

Examples:
She tried to calm the horse's excitement when it saw a snake in the road up ahead.
Through the man's excitement and gestures the firemen figured out that a child was still inside the burning building.

Mappings:
PropBank: NM
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 2

Sense Number 3: a feeling of sexual arousal

Commentary: EXCITEMENT[+state][+personal][+exhilaration][+arousal][+sexual]
NOTE: a narrow, specialized usage of Sense 1

Examples:
He felt no excitement at watching anonymous naked women dance on a stage.
They are afraid the teenagers will get too carried away by the excitement of heavy necking.

Examples:

The surfers talked about the excitement of riding the big waves on Oahu.
