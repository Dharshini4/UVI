blend-v; 1 Senses

Sense Number 1: Combine several elements to form an either temporary or permanent single whole; combine one element into another; harmonize.

Commentary: Includes: BLEND IN

Examples:
At this stage of making the cake, blend in the nuts.
The two types of paint blend well and do not separate.
We don't blend members of our two social groups.
Blend in some more sarcasm into an already sarcastic play and you've
got the movie version.
Their voices blended nicely.
This flavor will blend with those in your dish.
The sofa doesn't blend with the chairs.
Wow, Mark can really blend in with others.
Tigers use their stripes to blend in with the shadows of the forest.
