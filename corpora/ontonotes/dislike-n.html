dislike-n; 2 Senses

Sense Number 1: a feeling of aversion, distaste, antipathy

Commentary: DISLIKE[+state][+emotional][+aversion][+distaste]

Examples:
Bob developed a keen dislike for Pete after the incident.
The child's dislike of spinach went away over time.
The physicist has a general dislike of philosophers and sociologists.

Mappings:
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 1, 2

Sense Number 2: anything having a negative evaluation for someone

Commentary: DISLIKE[+entity][+role][+evaluation][+negative]

Examples:
I know John's likes and dislikes when it comes to food.
Mary's biggest dislike of the film was the ridiculous ending.

Mappings:
WordNet 0.0 Sense Numbers: 1a
