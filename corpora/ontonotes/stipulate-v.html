stipulate-v; 1 Senses

Sense Number 1: specify or demand a condition or requirement

Commentary: Syntax is: NP1 STIPULATE NP2 Syntax is: NP1[agent] STIPULATE TO

Examples:
The contract stipulates the dates of the payments.
The parties and their attorneys stipulate that the claims in this action be submitted to mediation.
They stipulated a date of payment and a price.
The divorcing parties cannot stipulate that the court be bound by the arbitrator's decision on child custody.
We have signed a contract which stipulates when the project must be completed.
The law stipulates that new cars must have seat belts for the driver and every passenger.
The State refused to stipulate to these facts.
We will stipulate to our receipt of all pertinent discovery documents, Your Honor.
Will the defendant stipulate her presence at the scene of the crime?
