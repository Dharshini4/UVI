ticket-n; 5 Senses

Sense Number 1: A commercial document showing that the holder is entitled to do something.

Commentary: TICKET[+document]

Examples:
I have tickets for the concert Friday.
Do you have a ticket to spare?

Mappings:
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 1

Sense Number 2: A label showing an owner, price, or other information about an object.

Commentary: TICKET[+label]

Examples:
She put tickets on all the inventory.
What does the ticket say about the price of the television?

Mappings:
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 2

Sense Number 3: A summons issued to an offender (especially to one who violates a traffic regulation)

Commentary: TICKET[+event][+law]

Examples:
The cop gave me a ticket for running the light.
I got a parking ticket yesterday.

Mappings:
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 3

Sense Number 4: A list of candidates nominated by a political party to run for election.

Commentary: TICKET[+political]

Examples:
Who's on the ticket this time?
We have a strong Democratic ticket.

Mappings:
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 4

Sense Number 5: The appropriate or desirable thing.

Commentary: TICKET[+desirable]

Examples:
This car could be just the ticket for a small family.
That's the ticket!

Mappings:
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 5
