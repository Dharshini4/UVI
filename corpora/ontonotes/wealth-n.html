wealth-n; 3 Senses

Sense Number 1: a state of affluence, being rich

Commentary: WEALTH[+state][+affluence][+material]

Examples:
John sought to make his wealth apparent to those around him.
It's mostly families of wealth that belong to the polo club.
He was dazzled by the woman's wealth and beauty.

Mappings:
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 1

Sense Number 2: an abundance or profusion of something

Commentary: WEALTH[+quantity][+abundance]

Examples:
This book contains a wealth of information on Wales.
She has such a wealth of musical talent.
They are poor but they have a wealth of friends and family.

Mappings:
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 2

Sense Number 3: property or assets with economic value

Commentary: WEALTH[+entity][+property][+utility][+economic]

Examples:
The newspaper mogul used his wealth to bribe officials and politicians.
They gave most of their wealth to museums and charities.
That country has great mineral wealth but little agricultural land or water.
John managed to double his personal wealth in two years.

Mappings:
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 3, 4
