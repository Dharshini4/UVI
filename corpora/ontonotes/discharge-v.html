discharge-v; 4 Senses

Sense Number 1: get rid of; empty; cause to expel

Commentary: Syntax Is:
NOTE: Can be concrete or abstract. NP1[agent] DISCHARGE[punctual] NP2[-animate,+/-concrete]) NP1[theme,+concrete] DISCHARGE

Examples:
Isn't it great that my loan was discharged?!
They discharged the stolen cargo in New York.
Tommy allegedly discharged the spray near a bathroom.
The prior proceedings discharged the Eastern pilot's claims against Continental.
The liquor is discharged and the dyeing is rinsed with fresh cold water.
The gun discharged.

Commentary:
NOTE: This sense includes the frames of military and medical facilities care. Syntax Is: NP1[agent] DISCHARGE[punctual] NP2[human] (from NP3[location,duty])

Sense Number 4: pronounce not guilty of a criminal charge

Commentary:
NOTE: This is a subsense of sense 2 referring specifically to a defendant being relieved of criminal charges.

Examples:
A Dhaka court yesterday discharged the editor and the publisher of
the daily Prothom Alo from a defamation case.
