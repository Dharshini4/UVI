stream-v; 5 Senses

Sense Number 1: Move in large numbers.

Examples:
People were streaming out of the theater.
The boys and the mob streamed together behind the two poor
journeymen.

Mappings:
VerbNet: appear-48.1.1
FrameNet: Mass_motion
PropBank: NM
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 3

Sense Number 2: Flow freely and abundantly. Includes metaphorical extensions.

Commentary: INCLUDES: STREAM OUT, STREAM DOWN Syntax includes: NP1[theme] STREAM PP

Examples:
Tears streamed down her face.
Rain streamed down outside.
About $60 million streamed out last week alone.

Mappings:
VerbNet: NM
FrameNet: Fluidic_motion
PropBank: NM
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 4, 5

Sense Number 3: Transmit live or continuously, as over the Internet.

Examples:
Apple could sell more Apple TVs if they streamed Steve Jobs' keynotes
live.
The movie streamed to thousands of computers before its official
release in theaters.

Mappings:
VerbNet: NM
FrameNet: NM
PropBank: NM

Sense Number 4: Extend, wave, or float outward, as if in the wind.

Examples:
Their manes streamed like still black pennants in the wind.
The sails streamed out and up, catching the breeze.

Mappings:
VerbNet: NM
FrameNet: NM
PropBank: NM

Sense Number 5: Exude profusely.

Commentary: Syntax is: NP1[source] STREAM NP2[product]

After Mary hit him, John's eyes streamed tears.
