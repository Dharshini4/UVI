fill-n; 4 Senses

Sense Number 1: a quantity sufficient for satisfaction

Commentary: FILL[+quantity][+amount][+sufficient][+for_satisfaction]

Examples:
The children ate their fill of candy on Halloween.
We could never get our fill of diving in the warm clear waters.
John said he's had his fill of surprised for one day. (ironic intent)

Mappings:
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 1

Sense Number 2: any substance used to fill a space or container

Commentary: FILL[+entity][+substance][+filler]

Examples:
You need more potting fill for this window box.
The fill in these pillows is some type of polyester.

Mappings:
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 2

Sense Number 3: the act of shading in color into an area

Commentary: FILL[+event][+shading_in][+color][+in_region]

Examples:
The artist's fill of the background with purple hues created a brooding effect.

Mappings:
WordNet 0.0 Sense Numbers: 1c

Sense Number 4: a short musical phrase performed by an instrument in a popular song

Commentary: FILL[+form][+musical][+phrase][+short][+of_instrument]

Examples:
I like the saxophone fill on that song between the verses.
He performed the piece with a drum fill at the end of the song.

Mappings:
WordNet 0.0 Sense Numbers: 1d
