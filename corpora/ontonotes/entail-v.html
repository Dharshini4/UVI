entail-v; 2 Senses

Sense Number 1: Involve as a necessary accompaniment or result; have as a logical consequence.

Examples:
The paper gave no details of what the restrictions would entail.
Indeed, for him inheritance entails immanence, but immanence does not
entail inheritance.
What does this move entail?

Mappings:

WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 1, 2

Sense Number 2: Limit the inheritance of property to a specific class of heirs.

Examples:
If I had property entailed upon me, I should be heir.
My father was the eldest son and the estates were entailed on him by custom.
