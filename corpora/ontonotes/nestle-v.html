nestle-v; 2 Senses

Sense Number 1: Actively position oneself for comfort or cause an animate being to be in such a position.

Examples:
We nestled against one another for warmth.
The children nestled into their sleeping bags.
The baby nestled her head in her mother's lap.
I nestled into the couch to watch television.
The rabbit nestled its head in my hands.

Sense Number 2: (Cause to) be positioned in a geographically sheltered location.

Examples:
We nestled our cabin deep in the forest.
The castle nestled in the mountains outside town.
