die-v; 11 Senses

Sense Number 1: expire; cease living or existing

Commentary: Includes: DIE OUT, DIE BACK, DIE OFF
NOTE: May be [transitive]--see example 4 Requires a physical entity that is dying, non-physical entities are ocvered by sense 9.

Examples:
The children died in the fire.
She died of cancer.
Martyrs die every day for their faith.
He died a gruesome death.
Whosoever believes in me shall never die!

Sense Number 2: lessen in intensity

Commentary: Includes: DIE DOWN
NOTE: The entity lessens, but this sense does not entail that the entity disappears, it can only be inferred that it exists in a lesser amount.

Examples:
The laughter died down.
The furor has died down, but the Dixie Chicks haven't.

Sense Number 9: disappearance, cessation of a non-physical entity

Commentary: Includes: DIE OUT, DIE BACK, DIE OFF, DIE AWAY
NOTE: May be used metaphorically--ceasing to exist relative to a context.

Examples:
My secret will die with me.
The words died on his lips.
Her desire died when she caught him picking his nose.
The storm died away.
The murmur of whispered conversations slowly died away as the jury
trudged back in.
The musician's voilin died away like a voice fading away in the
distance.
Slowly, the laughter died off.
