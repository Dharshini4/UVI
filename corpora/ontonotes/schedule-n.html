schedule-n; 5 Senses

Sense Number 1: an agenda or procedure for executing a plan

Commentary: SCHEDULE[+process][+agenda][+for_execution][+of_plan]

Examples:
Mary has a very hectic schedule to keep up with at work.
The manufacturer is behind schedule with this month's factory orders.
The schedule for making the launch to Mars cannot be altered by even one day.
Bob has come up with a tentative schedule for completing this project by year's end.

Mappings:
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 1

Sense Number 2: a published timetable

Commentary: SCHEDULE[+artifact][+document][+publication][+timetable]

Examples:
The bus schedule says the next bus leaves at 1:35.
The airline publishes a new flight schedule four times a year.
I discovered my train schedule was out of date.

Mappings:
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 2

Sense Number 3: a calendar of events

Commentary: SCHEDULE[+artifact][+document][+calendar][+of_events]

Examples:
Let's send each other copies of our schedules so we can coordinate when to meet.
The fall schedule of classes is posted on the college's website.
I don't see John's talk on the conference schedule of events.

Mappings:
WordNet 0.0 Sense Numbers: 1a

Sense Number 4: one of different forms to fill out on income tax returns

Commentary: SCHEDULE[+artifact][+document][+publication][+government][+tax_form]

Examples:
Do you have all the schedules you need to fill out your 2007 tax forms?
The schedule I'm missing is not Schedule A.

Mappings:
WordNet 0.0 Sense Numbers: 3

Sense Number 5: an appendix to a legal document

Commentary: SCHEDULE[+artifact][+document][+legal][+appendix]

Examples:
You will find the tables in a schedule at the end of the legal brief.
The attorney's assistant prepared the schedules that were included in the client's will.

Mappings:
WordNet 0.0 Sense Numbers: 2
