price-v; 2 Senses

Sense Number 1: decide or establish how much something costs

Commentary:
NOTE: Also includes the act of marking or labeling an item with a price.

Examples:
He priced the antique clock at $700.
Archives priced it at $19.95.
Look at these shoes that are priced at $900!
We spent the entire morning pricing the new merchandise.

Mappings:

WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 1

Sense Number 2: find out how much something costs

Examples:
I priced a few computers before deciding which one to buy.
The value of this is greater than when I priced it four years ago.
She spent last week pricing wedding dresses.
