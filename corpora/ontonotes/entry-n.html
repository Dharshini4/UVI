entry-n; 5 Senses

Sense Number 1: written record

Commentary: accounting entry, ledger entry - an item in a written record

Examples:
Suzy's diary entry tonight will be full of juicy gossip.
One credit entry can debit two budget categories.

Mappings:
PropBank: entry.02
WordNet 2.0 Sense Numbers: 1 3

Sense Number 2: appearance

Commentary: ENTRY[+event] introduction, debut, first appearance, launching, unveiling - the act of beginning something new

Examples:
They looked forward to the entry of their new product line.

Mappings:
PropBank: NM
WordNet 2.0 Sense Numbers: 2

Sense Number 3: submission

Commentary: something (manuscripts, architectural plans, models, estimates, or works of art) submitted for the judgement of others (as in a competition)

Examples:
Several of his entries were rejected by publishers.
What was the date of submission of your last entry.
She dropped her bake-off entry on the ground moments before the competition.

Mappings:
PropBank: entry.02
WordNet 2.0 Sense Numbers: 4

Sense Number 4: doorway

Commentary: entrance, entranceway, entryway, entree - something that provides access (entry or exit)

Examples:
The entrance to John's bedroom was only four feet tall because he was living in some else's storage space.
They waited at the entrance to the garden.
The entry was blocked by several large boxes.

Mappings:
PropBank: NM
WordNet 2.0 Sense Numbers: 5

Sense Number 5: act of entering

Commentary: ENTRY[+event] entrance, entering, ingress, incoming - the act of entering

Examples:
John's conspicuous entry diverted everyone's attention away from the teacher.
She made a grand entrance.

Mappings:
PropBank: entry.01
