liquify-v; 2 Senses

Sense Number 1: (Cause to) become a fluid, as when heated.

Examples:
Areas that are made of 'fill' tend to liquify.
Workers liquify the silver.

Sense Number 2: Sell, as in stock; make into a liquid (transferable) asset.

Examples:
The trust isn't commenting on when it might need to liquify its
Manville stock.
I decided to liquify my stock portfolio the other day.
