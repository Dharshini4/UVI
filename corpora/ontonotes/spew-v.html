spew-v; 2 Senses

Sense Number 1: Eject or send out in large quantities; also metaphorical.

Examples:
The volcano spews out molten rocks everyday.
The editors of the paper spew out hostile articles about the
Presidential candidate.
The photos showed active geysers spewing water and steam.
Protesters at the rally spewed bigoted remarks.

Sense Number 2: Expel or eject (saliva, phlegm, sputum, or vomit) from the mouth.

Examples:
He spewed continuously.
After drinking too much, the students spewed.
Grandfather spewed saliva as he spoke.
