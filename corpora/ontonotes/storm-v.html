storm-v; 3 Senses

Sense Number 1: Rain, hail, or snow hard and be very windy, often with thunder and lightning; blow hard.

Examples:
If it storms, we'll need shelter.
It's really storming outside - listen to the wind!

Mappings:
VerbNet: weather-57
FrameNet: Weather
PropBank: storm.01
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 3, 4

Sense Number 2: Take or occupy by force.

Commentary: INCLUDES: STORM THROUGH

Examples:
Police stormed through homes and confiscated valubles.
Angry men stormed the women's center and broke up the meeting.

Mappings:
VerbNet: NM
FrameNet: Attack
PropBank: storm.02
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 2, 5

Sense Number 3: Behave violently, as if in a state of great anger.

Commentary: INCLUDES: STORM OUT, STORM THROUGH, STORM AROUND

Examples:
The toddler stormed around the house.
Her husband stormed out the front door and never came back.
The chairman stormed through the lobby.

Mappings:
VerbNet: NM
FrameNet: Self_motion
PropBank: storm.02
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 1
