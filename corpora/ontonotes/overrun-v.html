overrun-v; 2 Senses

Sense Number 1: Invade or occupy in large numbers or in great amount.

Examples:
The roaches overran our kitchen.
The Kudzu plant overruns more of the South each day.
The Crusaders overran much of the Holy Land.

Sense Number 2: Surpass a landmark or limit.

Commentary:
NOTE: compare to sense 1, where the area filled is either geographical or political. Here is is a specific point in space or the confines of a container. Also, conquest is not involved.

Examples:
The plane overran the runway.
Water overran the edges of the glass.
