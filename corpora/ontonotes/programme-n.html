programme-n; 5 Senses

Sense Number 1: The physical brochure describing the details of a presentation or sporting event.

Commentary: PROGRAMME[+artifact]

Examples:
The programme this year was small and flimsy.
You can't tell the players without a programme.

Mappings:
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 1

Sense Number 2: An integrated course of academic studies.

Commentary: PROGRAMME[+academic]

Examples:
He was admitted to a new programme at the University.
My programme is highly competetive.

Mappings:
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 2

Sense Number 3: (computer science) a sequence of instructions that a computer can interpret and execute.

Commentary: PROGRAMME[+computer_science]

Examples:
The programme required several hundred lines of code.
I can't get my programme to run.

Mappings:
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 4

Sense Number 4: A system of projects, services, or steps that lead to a (public) goal.

Commentary: PROGRAMME[+event]

Examples:
The President has initiated a programme of reform.
They drew up a six-step programme.

Mappings:
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 5, 6

Sense Number 5: A performance or show.

Commentary: PROGRAMME[+performance][+event]

Examples:
Did you see his programme last night?
The programme lasted for more than two hours.

Mappings:
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 3, 7
