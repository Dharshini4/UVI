maunder-v; 2 Senses

Sense Number 1: wander aimlessly

Examples:
He maundered through life without a single ambition.
I can see her old grim visage now as she maundered about the kitchen.
They were maundering round with that old camera.
Those actors didn't know their parts; they maundered to and fro.
They maundered to their rental property.

Sense Number 2: talk indistinctly or senselessly

Commentary: Includes the VPC maunder on.

Examples:
He maundered terribly, all through his lecture.
He maundered on about the assassination of President Kennedy.
She maundered off into some nonsense about her yarn.
The way she was maundering made it difficult to understand her speech.
He maundered to himself, "the daughter of one of these politicians!"
