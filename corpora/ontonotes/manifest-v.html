manifest-v; 3 Senses

Sense Number 1: Provide evidence for; show by one's behavior, attitude, or external attributes; embody.

Commentary: Syntax Is: NP1 MANIFEST NP2[patient[abstract]]

Examples:
The buildings in Rome manifest a high level of architectural sophistication.
This decision manifests his sense of fairness.
She manifests all beauty and goodness.

Mappings:
VerbNet: reflexive_appearance-48.1.2
FrameNet: NP
PropBank: NM
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 1

Sense Number 2: Record in a ship's manifest.

Examples:
We manifested all the cargo by putting it into the ship's log.
Every passenger must be manifested by a member of the crew.

Mappings:
VerbNet: NM
FrameNet: NP
PropBank: NM
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 2

Sense Number 3: Come into existence; reveal its presence or make an apprearance.

Commentary: Syntax Includes: NP1 MANIFEST NP2[patient[concrete]] NP1[agent] MANIFEST
NOTE: May be reflexive.
NOTE: See final example. Unlike in Sense 1, the NP2 in Sense 2 becomes [concrete] by the action of the verb and thus may be considered concrete in the syntax.

Examples:
The ghost manifests each year on the same day.
The man manifested a large bulge near his liver, which doctors removed.
We have to manifest our goals.

Mappings:

FrameNet: NP
