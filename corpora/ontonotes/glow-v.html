glow-v; 2 Senses

Sense Number 1: Shine intensely; emit a steady, even light from a source.

Examples:
The fireflies were glowing and flying about in the garden.
The coals were glowing in the dark.
Their swords glowed red in the setting sun.
Shooting stars glowed across the sky.

Mappings:
VerbNet: light_emission-43.1
FrameNet: Location_of_light,Light_movement
PropBank: glow.01
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 1, 3

Sense Number 2: Have a complexion with a strong bright color, possibly as a result of an emotion; figuratively give off light.

Commentary:
NOTE: Light source must be human.

Examples:
People say you look like you're glowing when you leave a massage.
Her face glowed with happiness.
She was glowing with joy.
Make the people's hearts glow.
