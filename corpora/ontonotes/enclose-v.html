enclose-v; 2 Senses

Sense Number 1: Surround completely; close in, as if with a covering or border.

Examples:
Darkness enclosed him.
They enclosed the porch.
Fog enclosed the house.
The entire site is enclosed with an ornamental fence.
She enclosed his grave in barbed wire.

Mappings:
VerbNet: contiguous_location-47.8
FrameNet: NP
PropBank: enclose.01
WordNet 3.0 Sense Numbers: 1, 2, 3

Sense Number 2: Introduce or submit, as to a bank machine or organization.

Commentary:
NOTE: Must include or imply a PATH and probably a RECIPIENT.

Examples:
I have enclosed a check for tuition and fees.
Please enclose bank information and submit below.
