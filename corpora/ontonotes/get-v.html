Sense Number 4: cause to be/do

Commentary: For this sense, the subject is a cause of a change, not the undergoer. Syntax is: NP[Agent] V NP[Patient] ADJP[Result] NP[Agent] V NP[Patient] S[Result] NP[Agent] V NP[Patient] PP[Result]

Examples:
He got his squad on the ball.
The children finally got me to buy a computer.
You don't wanna get me mad.

Commentary: For this sense, the subject is the undergoer and the final state must be realised with a "to" preposition. Syntax is: NP[Patient] V to-PP[Result]

Sense Number 11: Light verb usage - undergo

Commentary: Tag all usages where "get" isn't the main predicate--it accompanies a NOUN that expresses the event semantis. The only semantics to "get" are likely to be those that indicate that the subject is an undergoer of the main event.

Examples:
He got an insulin shock after eating too much candy.
The other fighter got a punch to the face.
I just got surgery last week.

Sense Number 12: Auxiliary usage--start, able to

Commentary: Tag all usages where "get" isn't the main predicate--it accompanies VERB that expresses the event semantics. The only semantics to "get" are likely to be those that indicate that the main event is starting, or with certain infinitives, that the subject has the privilege of doing the main event.

Examples:
Get working as soon as the sun rises.
Get going!
You have to mail in a couple of forms before you get to vote.
I get to go home in one week!
