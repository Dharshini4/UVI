step-n; 11 Senses

Sense Number 1: nonspatial progress

Commentary: any nonspatial maneuver made as part of progress toward a goal

Examples:
The situation called for strong measures.
The police took steps to reduce crime.

Sense Number 2: distance

Commentary: footstep, pace, stride - the distance covered by a step (used to measure length)

Examples:
He walked off ten steps from the old tree and began to dig.
The box was buried fifteen steps from the corner of the garage.
The treasure map's form of measurements were in steps.

PropBank: NM
WordNet 2.0 Sense Numbers: 2

Sense Number 3: act of stepping

Commentary: STEP[+event] the act of changing location by raising the foot and setting it down

Examples:
His steps were unsteady.
His ankle hurt more with each step.
Taking those five steps took all of his courage.
He took ten minutes to walk those five steps.
