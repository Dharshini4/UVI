diversify-v; 1 Senses

Sense Number 1: make or become varied or different

Examples:
The echinoids diversified into many types throughout the Jurassic period
Millions of years ago, changes in climate caused animal and plant life to diversify.
The company has diversified into fruit juices and natural drinks, such as gazpacho.
The primary objective of free-choice studies is to diversify studies.
As competitors entered the market they decided to diversify the menu.
