camp-v; 2 Senses

Sense Number 1: Live in or as if in a tent; stay in an area.

Commentary: Includes: CAMP OUT, CAMP DOWN

Examples:
Can we camp again this summer?
The houseguests had to camp in the living room.
We can camp down here tonight.
The children always want to camp out.
He camped out at a high-tech center waiting for stocks to move.

Sense Number 2: Give a performative affectation to (something).

Commentary: Includes: CAMP UP, CAMP IT UP

Examples:
Some later productions have tended to camp up the show.
Or do you just long to camp up your life?
Camp it up everybody!
